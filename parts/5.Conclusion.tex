\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this paper, we explored the problem of sound speed field (SSF) estimation using travel-time information, focusing on inversion in large-scale water environments. We presented the theory behind the well-established travel-time tomography technique, along with the relevant underlying mathematical definitions. We also introduced a novel approach for SSF estimation based on the singular value decomposition (SVD), including its associated framework and algebraic developments.

The two techniques were compared under two relevant scenarios: one aligned with common configurations in the literature, and another reflecting more practical constraints. The results show that both techniques achieve similar and adequate performance in the literature-based scenario, but exhibit unsatisfactory performance in the practical scenario. In this case, the proposed method provides a marginal improvement in SSF estimation, although the overall results remain inadequate.

These findings indicate that the practical scenario lacks sufficient spatial information, leading to overfitting in the estimation of the inverse map. Consequently, neither technique, as currently formulated, is suitable for this configuration.
