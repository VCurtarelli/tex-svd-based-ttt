\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
In this paper, we explored the problem of sound speed field (SSF) estimation using travel-time information, focused on the inversion in large scale water environments. We presented the theory for the well established travel-time tomography technique, along with the relevant underlying mathematical definitions. We also presented a novel technique for SSF estimation, based on the singular value decomposition (SVD), along with the relevant framework and related algebraic developments. These two techniques were compared in two relevant scenarios: one closer to the commonly found in the literature; and one more aligned with the practical motivations. The results show that both techniques had similar and adequate performance in the literature-based scenario, but had unsatisfactory results in the practical scenario, with the proposed method being marginally better in estimating the SSF. This leads to the conclusion that the practical scenario lacks sufficient spatial information, leading to overfitting in the minimization process.
